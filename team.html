    VTOL fix wing model 垂直起落固定翼

    Wing span 1.3m, gross weight 1.6kg, powered by two 1kg duct fans. Operates in a similar way to F35 fighter jet. Auto-balanced by an Arduino single board computer with an accelerometer measuring angular velocity about three axes.

    Project starts from Dec 2010, expected to end in March 2012. Total investiment under 5K RMB. Four undergrad students participated in this project, team leader is Li Zhuoyue.

    Further reading: the DUT F35; a video of bench testing

    Autonomous aerial vehicle

    Fix wing aircraft with an Ardupilot auto-pilot system, this is an open source hardware system that can work with regular R/C controller, servos and GPS modules. This project starts from March 2011, expected to ends in May 2012. The goal is to take off from west end of DUT campus, flies autonomouly to the Aerodynamics Lab which locates at the east end of the campus, drops a bottle of mineral water and returns to the taking-off location. One grad student and two undergrad students work on this project. Team leader is Liu Xuanhe.

    This project is supported by the National Undergraduate Creativity Plan, 10,000 Yuan 入选国家级大学生创新实验计划。

    Regular R/C controlled models

    Regular R/C models powered by an electrical motor or an internal combustion engine. Year 1 student build models using foam boards, Years 2,3 students use woods. Model team also offer free trainning courses on model building to year 1 students from any department in the university. The trainning team is led by Yuan Qihang.
